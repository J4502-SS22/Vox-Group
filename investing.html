Investing

When someone invests, they are distributing resources, like money, with the hope of generating a profit. Whether it's to build up the value of something and sell it later or to generate income and build wealth gradually over time. Some of the different forms of investing include the stock market, bonds, real estate, commodities, collectibles, savings accounts and funds.

It is also important to understand return and risk. Wells Fargo Advisors say that return is the money or value you get back when you invest money into something. Risk is the possibility of receiving a lowered value from the investment.

In this section, we will introduce the different forms of investing, such as the stock market and cryptocurrency, along with providing some helpful tools and tips. Whether you want to get serious about investing or just want to get a general grasp of it, this page and the resources provided will be helpful in informing your investing decisions.

Helpful Links:

Robin Hood

what is Robin Hood?

Robin Hood aims to help you build your portfolio as you invest in stocks, options, and ETFs at a low cost. They also help you manage cryptocurrency, keeping track of the market and offering a place to easily keep track of the rates.

Wells Fargo

what is Wells Fargo Investing?

The Wells Fargo Investing App offers four different ways for you to invest your money, including doing it on your own, automated investing, an investing advisor and a team that helps balance your wealth. The app will provide different resources for you to make informed wealth management and investing decisions.

Ellevest

what is Ellevest?

Ellevest is a company run by women for women. They offer services such as private wealth management, digital investing, and money and career coaching. They also create an investing portfolio for you, using stocks, bonds, and alternative funds, taking you through the steps for effective investments.
